My August Reads

Mornings in Jenin by Susan Abulhawa

Mornings in Jenin is a generational, historical fiction novel following the displacement of a Palestinian family from Ein Hod during the 1948 Nakba. I must caveat that whilst the characters in this story are purely fictious, the suffering they face is fuelled by real life events. Abulhawa has taken extreme care to ensure that the political and social events that spin the trajectory of the characters' lives in this book are in fact true events.

This book has been on my TBR list for quite some time after one of my close friends, Zoë, had been insisting that I read it. Having only read at best a few historical novels I can't even call myself a dilitante when it comes to the genre. However, after reading this book I was quick to pick up another historical fiction novel, so perhaps things are changing.

"Mornings in Jenin is hands down one of my favourite reads of 2023." - Zoë, 2023
